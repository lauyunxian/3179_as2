Income Levels and Inequality Across Countries

The map highlights Luxembourg, which is the richest country, with a GDP per capita of $190,000, and Burundi, which is the poorest, with just $231. This big difference highlights how unevenly wealth is spread around the world. The huge gap in GDP between these two countries demonstrates the drastic differences in economic situations globally.

Countries with higher HDI usually have lower Gini scores, meaning their income is more evenly spread. European countries mostly fall into the high HDI and low inequality group, while many African countries tend to have lower HDI and higher inequality.

The trend also suggests that as a country develops and improves its HDI, it often also works towards reducing income inequality.

The State of Income Inequality in Malaysia

With the average income in Malaysia being RM 7,903, it's clear that most states fall short of this benchmark. In contrast, those with incomes exceeding RM 10,000 reveal a troubling level of income inequality, indicating that many are left behind and struggling to make ends meet.

Moreover, Sabah's highest poverty rate highlights that even in regions with higher average incomes, many individuals still live below the poverty line.
